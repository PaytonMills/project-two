Behind the Spin: A DJ's Experience with WXYC 89.3

A Web Story Exclusive by Payton Mills

Audrey Walsh is a rising sophomore at UNC Chapel Hill. She is studying to be a Journalism major at the Hussman School of Journalism and Media. Audrey has been working at WXYC since the first day of her freshman year. Her radio show is broadcasted every Friday from 8 to 10 AM.

“WXYC is a DJ-oriented environment where we always get to express ourselves” – Audrey Walsh

Audrey’s musical experience began when she was very young. Her first memories of music involve an obsession with Elvis Pressley, and she accredits this fascination as the influence behind her love of older music. During her broadcast, Audrey likes to play compilations of world-psychedelic music, especially from African-inspired collections. She says it is “great to hear these experiences and viewpoints of people not typically represented in Western music”.

“When we have the opportunity as DJs to pull from physical music collections, we get the stories behind these pieces where we see notes left by previous DJs or musicians.” – Audrey Walsh

Audrey used to work for the “Melted” music magazine as a content creator and writer, attending and reporting on concerts and local indie shows. She is also involved in the local music scene in Chapel Hill, and she frequently attends house shows and concerts in the area. Audrey recently started making music of her own by drawing on inspiration from other local North Carolina musicians.
